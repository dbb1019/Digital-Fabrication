Computer-Controlled Cutting
 

Week 4

There are different types of machines that can be used for computer-controlled cutting. Waterjet cutting uses high-pressure water flow or an abrasive water stream to cut materials, making it suitable for metals, glass, ceramics, and other materials that are not heat-resistant. Vinyl cutting is mainly used for flexible materials such as stickers, films, and textiles. It can be used for trademarks, packaging, etc. Laser cutting uses a high-energy laser beam to melt or vaporize materials. It can be used to cut wood, arcrylic, and fabrics. It can cut complex patterns precisely. We can also use laser cutting to engrave the material like wood, it is like drawing something on the material.

Laser cutting uses a high-energy laser beam focused on the material surface, and causes the material to rapidly melt, burn, or vaporize, and the airflow extracts the molten residue. Laser Waist refers to the narrowest part of the laser beam near the focal point, affecting cutting precision. Focal point is the most concentrated and powerful point of the laser beam, located on or within the material. Kerf Width refers to the width of the material removed during laser cutting, affecting the fit of the final components. In FreeCAD, I used Spreadsheet to set parameters such as material thickness, kerf width, and egg diameter. First, I created a Part, then added a Body, and drew a hollow cylinder. Then I improvised the sketch for the support structure. I then rotated this support structure 90 degrees to create another support. For the laser cutting, I used Inkscape on the Fab Lab computer to open the PDF file, but there were many overlapping lines inside. However, after deleting them, I was still unable to print properly. After a long debugging process, we decided to open the PDF file with Adobe Illustrator. Since I was only cutting, I removed all fills and set the stroke to black with a width of 0.01mm. Before the actual cutting, I first cut a rectangle as a test to adjust the appropriate speed and ensure that the 4mm plywood board could be cut through. Then, I proceeded with the final cutting. I used the tested parameters, but the wood board did not cut through. After that, I lowered the speed, and the cut was successful.
