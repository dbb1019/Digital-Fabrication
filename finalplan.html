Final Project

Concept

This project explores the intersection of sound, robotics, and ritual through the creation of a mechanical sound installation based on a singing bowl. Inspired by the aesthetics of a phonograph, the device uses two motorized actuators: one to gently rub the rim of the bowl, and another to strike it at controlled intervals. By precisely modulating speed, pressure, and rhythm, the machine can produce a range of resonant tones and percussive gestures, mimicking human interaction with the instrument. The goal is to create a meditative, semi-autonomous sound sculpture that bridges traditional acoustic resonance with mechatronic control.

Main Challenges

One of the main challenges in this project is achieving expressive sound control through mechanical motion. Unlike traditional digital sound synthesis, working with a physical singing bowl introduces complexities such as resonance timing, friction sensitivity, and precise actuation. Another challenge is balancing engineering precision with artistic intent. This project is not just a technical build, but also an exploration of how machines can perform subtle, meditative actions that are usually associated with human presence. Through this process, I hope to learn more about sound-making through physical means. More than anything, I want to create something meaningful and a little unusual‚Äîsomething that resonates both literally and metaphorically.
